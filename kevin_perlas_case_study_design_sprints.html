Case Study: Design Sprints  ·  ServiceNow Workflow Design Studio  ·  2026
When you have a complex or novel business challenge and need alignment and validation in addition to ideas, a design sprint can work wonders.
THE SITUATION
ServiceNow needed to prove to Visa's VP of Product and Risk that we could be the orchestration layer between their back-end systems and a new unified front end for internal and external risk management teams. Visa's teams were located across the globe, were juggling 11+ legacy systems, and had different measures of success. I needed to understand the underlying problems, align stakeholders around a shared vision, and partner with the architect and developer to create a compelling solution concept. The big challenge: this typically takes us 10-12 weeks, but the executive pitch was in 6.
MY APPROACH
Communicate clearly + set and maintain expectations + leverage AI. In our kickoff, I communicated the risks and dependencies to keep us on track towards delivery, so everyone knew the stakes. I began discovery interviews the same day and facilitated a rolling synthesis through our discovery phase so scope and architecture always reflected the evolving body of research. We entered our 2-day co-innovation workshop prepared and left with everything the architect and designer needed to build the concept. Visa got the same depth of discovery and the same fidelity of solution, in half the typical time, made possible in part by AI-assisted synthesis tooling I built specifically for this purpose.
An example of a design sprint output can be a future state visualization
INSIGHTS AND FACTORS
  • Most fraud decisions can be automated, but when analysts are required, they need data from multiple sources in seconds. The system of the future augments that data with AI-driven decision support that indicates urgency, priority, outliers, and defensible recommendations.
  • Our demo couldn't just show features. It needed to visualize a holistic vision for a more efficient, integrated way a fraud case could flow between systems and people until resolution.
  • The AI tool chain I built specifically for design sprints accelerated and automated tasks that were previously manual and time-consuming, improved data retention and traceability, and accelerated delivery.
OUTCOMES
Following the executive demonstration, Visa's VP of Product and Risk concluded with, “We're all in.” The account team received a verbal commitment to move forward, validating both the solution vision and the accelerated discovery approach. It was also their first design sprint, and they committed to using the approach again in future engagements.
My Specific Contribution
  • Was the primary point of contact for our account team and the client and led the solution presentation.
  • Created the project plan, ran discovery interviews, planned and facilitated the on-site workshop, and led data synthesis.
  • Created the AI toolchain that ingested, managed, synthesized, and tracked data and insights, while adhering to human-in-the-loop reviews and gateways.
  • Guided the architect and developer from research through concept creation.
What Changed
50% faster
12-week engagement compressed to 6, without cutting scope or sacrificing solution fidelity.
16 interviews  / 2 days
Interviews across the US, UK, Brazil, and Australia and 2 workshop days in Atlanta required rigor and discipline.
Commitment secured
Visa moved from evaluation to a purchasing commitment. Their decision-maker commented "We're all in" on the solution.
